# Blog Post Brainstorm: The Software Content Creator

## Core Thesis
Coding agents are rapidly making software trivially reproducible. When building software stops being the hard part, the value shifts from *implementation* to *taste, product vision, and audience understanding*. This mirrors what happened to video/film when the iPhone + YouTube democratized content creation.

## Motivation & Angle
- This is **not** a promotion for the RevEng project. RevEng is a technical/academic project; this blog is a philosophical companion piece.
- The tone is **essayistic and curious**, not argumentative or didactic. Thinking out loud, not trying to convince. "Here's what I notice, here's what I wonder."
- Similar to how releasing SWE-bench two years ago made it hard to envision 90% performance, but here we are — what does it *mean* when agents get that good?
- Not trying to comfort or alarm. Just genuinely projecting what the shift looks like concretely, beyond the "people will lose their jobs" narrative.

## Key Ideas

### Software stops being a secret
- Tech companies were valuable because the technology itself was closed-source and hard to replicate.
- RevEng demonstrates the thesis concretely: an agent observes a black-box system and reconstructs it from scratch.
- When an agent can observe behavior and recreate the implementation, *code* is no longer the moat.

### The moat shifts, not disappears
- Code becomes commodity. What remains defensible: taste, product judgment, data, network effects, community, trust.
- The barrier to *entering the arena* drops to near zero. The game shifts from "can you build it" to "can you win users."
- A scrappy team of 3 can build a technically competitive alternative to products that currently require 500 engineers. They compete on product vision and niche targeting.

### The creator economy parallel
- Hollywood studios : big SaaS companies (massive capital, large teams, gatekept distribution)
- iPhone + YouTube : coding agents + app stores / web distribution
- YouTubers / TikTok creators : software content creators / indie hackers
- Movie attendance decline : enterprise SaaS churn to niche alternatives
- **Key irony**: People consume *more* video than ever, but theaters are declining. Total demand for software will go *up*, but it fragments into a long tail of niche products.

### The "10 Airbnbs" vision
- Current SaaS is one-size-fits-all. Airbnb looks roughly the same for every user.
- When building a full-stack app becomes trivial, it becomes economically viable to build for a 10,000-person niche that Airbnb would never prioritize.
- It's not just aesthetic customization — it's fundamentally different product decisions, policies, trust models.
- The long tail becomes economically viable.

### Where the analogy breaks / honest scoping
- Network effects and data moats don't disappear just because code is cheap to produce.
- Certain categories of software (platforms with strong two-sided markets, regulated industries) are more resilient.
- This primarily disrupts: tooling, productivity apps, vertical SaaS with thin data moats.
