# Bon Iver / Justin Vernon Origin Story - Research Document

Compiled research for blog post on Bon Iver's origin as a parallel to music production democratization.

---

## 1. THE SETUP: Life Crisis and Retreat to the Cabin

### DeYarmond Edison Breakup
- **Band formed**: 2002 in Eau Claire, Wisconsin (evolved from Vernon's earlier band Mount Vernon)
- **Members**: Justin Vernon (guitar/vocals), Brad Cook (bass/guitar), Phil Cook (keyboards/banjo), Joe Westerlund (percussion), Chris Porterfield
- **The move**: Band relocated from Eau Claire to **Raleigh, North Carolina** to pursue music careers
- **Breakup announced**: **September 15, 2006**
- **Cause**: "Tensions caused by different musical directions and philosophies and personal reasons." Vernon was effectively kicked out during his illness period
- **Post-breakup**: Remaining members formed **Megafaun**; Porterfield later formed **Field Report**
- Source: [DeYarmond Edison - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DeYarmond_Edison)
- Source: [Jagjaguwar - DeYarmond Edison](https://jagjaguwar.com/artist/deyarmond-edison/)
- Source: [SPIN - DeYarmond Edison Epoch](https://www.spinmagazine.com/2023/06/deyarmond-edison-epoch/)

### Vernon's Personal Crisis (Triple Blow)
1. **Illness**: Severe mononucleosis that damaged his liver (liver infection)
2. **Band dissolution**: Removed from the band by members he'd known since adolescence
3. **Romantic breakup**: Relationship ended during this same period
- Source: [The Boar - For Emma Retrospective](https://theboar.org/2023/04/for-emma-forever-ago-retrospective/)
- Source: [Wikipedia - For Emma, Forever Ago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/For_Emma,_Forever_Ago)

### The Cabin
- **Location**: Northwestern Wisconsin, approximately **one hour northwest of Eau Claire**
- **Property**: **80-acre rural area** owned by Vernon's father, referred to as "the land"
- **Structure**: Alpine-style, timber-frame cabin; Vernon's father had replaced the dirt floor and installed plumbing prior to the recording period
- **Setting**: Remote hunting cabin in Wisconsin's woods, winter conditions
- **Notable detail**: Vernon had an encounter where he had to frighten a bear from his porch before the creative work began in earnest
- Source: [Wikipedia - For Emma, Forever Ago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/For_Emma,_Forever_Ago)
- Source: [The Boar - For Emma Retrospective](https://theboar.org/2023/04/for-emma-forever-ago-retrospective/)

## 2. THE CREATION: Recording For Emma, Forever Ago

### Timeline
- **Recording period**: **November 2006 - January 2007** (approximately three months of isolation)
- Vernon initially went to the cabin with no intention of making an album; was unproductive at first

### Equipment (Remarkably Minimal)
- **Computer**: Power Mac G4
- **DAW**: Pro Tools LE (limited "M-Powered" version)
- **Audio interface**: Digidesign Mbox
- **Microphone**: **Shure SM57** dynamic cardioid microphone (~$100 retail)
- **Guitars**: Multiple, including a **1964 Sears Silvertone guitar** (which he had repaired by trading venison to a local luthier)
- **Other instruments**: Synthesizer
- **Software**: Auto-Tune (used for vocal manipulation/harmonies, not pitch correction - became a signature aesthetic element)
- **Total**: Essentially one ~$100 microphone, a computer, basic software, and guitars
- Source: [MusicRadar - How Bon Iver made a Platinum-selling record with two guitars and a microphone](https://www.musicradar.com/news/bon-iver-platinum-record-microphone)
- Source: [Sage Audio - Two Critically Acclaimed Albums Recorded with $100 Microphone](https://www.sageaudio.com/articles/two-critically-acclaimed-albums-recorded-100-microphone)
- Source: [Wikipedia - For Emma, Forever Ago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/For_Emma,_Forever_Ago)

### Creative Process
- Recorded **nine tracks** depicting pain, depression, and loneliness
- Layered guitars, vocals, and horns all through the single SM57 microphone
- Features distinctive **falsetto vocal harmonies** (notably layered extensively on "Lump Sum")
- Dynamic range from restrained acoustic pieces to chaotic emotional eruptions
- Vernon initially viewed the recordings as **rough demos** for later re-recording
- After sharing with friends and seeing their reaction, he decided to release them with only minor modifications
- Added echoing trumpets on the title track to enhance the sense of isolation
- The constraints of recording alone with minimal gear became the album's **defining aesthetic quality** -- the lo-fi intimacy, the layered self-harmonies, the organic imperfections
- Source: [The Boar - For Emma Retrospective](https://theboar.org/2023/04/for-emma-forever-ago-retrospective/)
- Source: [Stereophile - Recording of May 2008](https://www.stereophile.com/content/recording-may-2008-emma-forever-ago)

### Key Quote on Equipment
> "Justin Vernon's For Emma, Forever Ago is the ultimate proof that it's not what you have, it's what you do with it." -- MusicRadar
- Source: [MusicRadar](https://www.musicradar.com/news/bon-iver-platinum-record-microphone)

## 3. THE BREAKTHROUGH: From 500 CDs to Grammy Winner

### Initial Self-Release
- **MySpace upload**: **June 1, 2007** (first public availability)
- **Physical release date**: **July 8, 2007**
- **Format**: CD, **limited run of 500 copies**
- **Release event**: Party at **House of Rock** in Eau Claire, Wisconsin
- **Distribution plan**: Vernon planned to send 100 copies to press outlets
- Source: [Wikipedia - For Emma, Forever Ago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/For_Emma,_Forever_Ago)

### Blog-Driven Discovery (Word of Mouth)
- **My Old Kentucky Blog**: First major blog to popularize the record (June 2007)
- **BrooklynVegan**: Positive coverage within weeks
- **Pitchfork**: Positive review in October 2007
- **Stereogum**: Coverage following CMJ Music Marathon appearance
- **CMJ Music Marathon**: Performance in **October 2007** generated significant label interest
- Source: [Popular Timelines - Bon Iver](https://populartimelines.com/timeline/Bon-Iver/full)
- Source: [Wikipedia - For Emma, Forever Ago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/For_Emma,_Forever_Ago)

### Label Signing & Re-Release
- **Signed with Jagjaguwar**: **October 29, 2007**
- **US re-release**: **February 19, 2008** (Jagjaguwar)
- **UK/Europe release**: **May 12, 2008** (4AD)
- **Debut week sales** (Feb 2008 re-release): 4,000 copies
- Source: [Popular Timelines - Bon Iver](https://populartimelines.com/timeline/Bon-Iver/full)

### Critical Reception
- **Metacritic**: 88/100 (27 critics)
- **Pitchfork**: 8.1/10
- **AllMusic**: 5/5 stars
- **Rolling Stone**: 3.5/5 stars
- **The Guardian**: 4/5 stars
- **The A.V. Club**: A-

### Chart Performance
- US Billboard 200: peaked at **#64**
- US Top Heatseekers: **#1**
- US Independent Albums: **#4**
- UK Albums Chart: **#42**
- Australian Albums: **#32**
- Source: [Wikipedia - For Emma, Forever Ago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/For_Emma,_Forever_Ago)

### Sales & Certifications
- US Nielsen SoundScan (by June 2011): **335,000 copies**
- **RIAA Platinum** (2017): 1,000,000 combined units
- **UK (BPI) Platinum**: 300,000+ units
- **Australia (ARIA) Platinum**: 70,000 copies
- **Denmark**: 2x Platinum (40,000 units)
- Vinyl sales (first half of 2010s): over 102,000 copies
- Source: [Wikipedia - For Emma, Forever Ago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/For_Emma,_Forever_Ago)

### Album Rankings
- **Rolling Stone's 500 Greatest Albums of All Time** (2020 edition): #461
- **Pitchfork's Top 200 Albums of the 2000s**: #29
- **Observer Music Monthly** (2008): #1 in 50 Albums of the Year
- **Rough Trade** (2008): #1 in Top 50 Albums

### Grammy Awards (54th Annual, February 12, 2012)
- **Venue**: Staples Center, Los Angeles
- **Won**: Best New Artist
- **Won**: Best Alternative Music Album (for self-titled *Bon Iver* album)
- **Presenters**: Carrie Underwood and Tony Bennett
- **Other nominees for Best New Artist**: The Band Perry, J. Cole, **Nicki Minaj**, Skrillex
- **Notable**: Considered an upset win over heavily favored Nicki Minaj
- **Broadcast**: CBS
- Source: [Grammy.com - Bon Iver wins Best New Artist](https://www.grammy.com/videos/bon-iver-wins-best-new-artist)

### Key Milestone: Late Show with David Letterman
- **December 11, 2008**: Performed "Skinny Love" on Late Show with David Letterman (major mainstream exposure)

## 4. COLLABORATIONS: From Cabin to World Stage

### Kanye West
- West was an admirer of *For Emma, Forever Ago*
- **2009**: West invited Vernon to Hawaii studio sessions for *My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy*
- **Tracks**: "Monster," "Dark Fantasy" (backing vocals), "Lost in the World"
- Extended to *Watch the Throne* (2011) and *Yeezus* (2013)
- **2011**: Vernon performed with West at Coachella (*Billboard* called it the festival's best performance)
- West later recorded at Vernon's **April Base studio** in Wisconsin for *The Life of Pablo*
- Collaboration ended ~2019 over personal/political differences
- Source: [NickiSwift - What Happened Between Justin Vernon and Kanye West](https://www.nickiswift.com/229786/what-happened-between-bon-ivers-justin-vernon-and-kanye-west/)

### Taylor Swift
- **"exile"** (featuring Bon Iver) released **August 3, 2020** from Taylor Swift's *Folklore* album
- Song originated during pandemic isolation; producer Aaron Dessner forwarded demo to Vernon
- **Chart**: Debuted at #6 on US Hot 100; reached #3 in Australia/Ireland
- Became Bon Iver's **highest-charting and first top-ten song** across all markets
- **December 10, 2020**: Featured on Taylor Swift's *Evermore* title track
- Nominated for Best Pop Duo/Group Performance at 63rd Grammy Awards
- Source: [Wikipedia - Exile (song)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exile_(song))

## 5. BROADER CONTEXT: Music Production Democratization

### The Lo-Fi / Bedroom Recording Movement (2000s)
- Indie artists "embraced a more lo-fi aesthetic, often recording their music in small studios or even their own bedrooms"
- DIY approach created intimate quality that resonated with listeners fatigued by overproduced mainstream tracks
- The broader cultural movement included The Strokes, Arcade Fire, The White Stripes, Death Cab for Cutie
- Internet and music blogs became critical discovery mechanisms, bypassing traditional radio/label gatekeepers
- Source: [Yellowbrick - The Rise of 2000s Indie Music](https://www.yellowbrick.co/blog/music/the-rise-of-2000s-indie-music-exploring-a-unique-sound)
- Source: [SampleFocus - Lo-Fi Movement Timeline](https://blog.samplefocus.com/blog/the-intriguing-timeline-behind-the-lo-fi-movement/)

### Music Production Cost Data
- **1970s**: A four-track reel machine cost ~$10,000 (equivalent to a small car)
- **1980s**: Affordable cassette four-track recorders made home recording accessible for the first time
- **1990s**: Digital Audio Workstations (DAWs) became widely available, dramatically reducing costs
- **2000s+**: "All you needed was a laptop or even your phone to start making music"
- **Key quote**: "The digital revolution has completely changed music production. It shifted the power to make music from big studios to independent artists, making it easier and more affordable for people to record and produce their own music."
- Source: [YouTalent - Historical Overview of Home Recording Studios](https://blog.youtalent.com/historical-overview-home-recording-studios-highlighting-technological-advances-their-impact-music-pro)

### Recording Studio Industry (Current)
- Global recording studio market: **$6.8 billion in 2023**, growing at 5.2% CAGR
- **35% of independent musicians** recorded their debut albums in home studios (as of 2023)
- Residential facilities now represent **28% of the global market share**
- **78% of professional studios** use DAWs as primary tools
- Source: [World Metrics - Recording Studio Industry Statistics](https://worldmetrics.org/recording-studio-industry-statistics/)

### The Bon Iver Parallel (For the Blog Post)
Vernon's story is the perfect case study:
- **Old model**: Recording studio (like a large engineering team) with expensive gear, producers, engineers, label backing
- **New model**: One person, ~$100 microphone, a computer, basic software, a cabin
- **Result**: Platinum album, Grammy winner, collaborator with the biggest artists in the world
- **The differentiator was not resources -- it was taste, vision, and emotional authenticity**
- The SM57 microphone retails for ~$100. The Mbox interface was entry-level. Pro Tools LE was the budget version. Total recording setup was likely under $1,000-2,000.
- A professional studio album in the mid-2000s could easily cost $50,000-500,000+
